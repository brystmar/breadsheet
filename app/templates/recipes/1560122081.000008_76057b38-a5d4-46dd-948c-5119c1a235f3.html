Pain de Campagne (Sourdough)

★★★★★

Bread


Prep Time: 24 hrs
Cook Time: 55 min
Difficulty: Medium
Servings: 2 loaves
Source: Flour Water Salt Yeast


Ingredients

Waking Up Levain:

200 g refrigerated starter

100 g whole wheat flour

400 g white flour

400 g water @ 95°F


Feeding Levain:

100 g mature, active starter @ room temp

100 g whole wheat flour

400 g white flour

400 g water @ 85-90°F


Final Dough:

60 g whole wheat flour

740 g white flour

620 g water @ 90-95°F

21 g fine, non-iodized sea salt

2 g instant dried yeast (½ tsp)

360 g levain

Directions

1a. Feed the levain: About 24hr after your previous feeding of the levain, discard all but 100g of the levain, leaving the remainder in your 6qt tub. Add 400g of white flour, 100g of whole wheat flour, and 400g of water @ 85-90F and mix by hand just until incorporated. Cover and let rest at room temperature for 6-8 hours before mixing the final dough.

1b. Autolyse: After 6-8 hours, mix the 740g white flour and the 60g whole wheat flour by hand in a 12qt round tub. Add the 620g of 90-95°F water and mix by hand just until incorporated. Cover and let rest for 20-30 minutes.

2. Mix the Final Dough: Sprinkle the 21g salt and the 2g (1/2 tsp) of yeast evenly over the top of the dough. Put a container with about a finger's depth of warm water on your scale so you can easily remove the levain after it's weighed. With wet hands, transfer 360g of levain into the container. Transfer the weighed levain to the 12qt dough tub, minimizing the amount of water transferred with it. Mix by hand, wetting your working hand before mixing so the dough doesn't stick to you. Use the pincer method (page 67) alternating with folding the dough to fully integrate the ingredients. The target dough temperature at the end of the mix is 77-78°F.

3. Fold: This dough needs 3-4 folds (see pages 69-70). It's easiest to apply the folds during the first 1½ to 2 hours after mixing the dough. When the dough is about 2½ times its original volume, about 5 hours after mixing, it's ready to be divided.

4. Divide: With floured hands, gently ease the dough out of the tub and onto a lightly floured work surface. With your hands still floured, pick up the dough and ease it back down onto the work surface in a somewhat even shape. Use a bit of flour to dust the area in the middle where you'll cut the dough, then cut it into 2 equal-size pieces with a dough cutter.

5. Shape: Dust 2 proofing baskets with flour. Shape each piece of dough into a medium-tight ball following the instructions on pages 71-73. Place each seam side down in its proofing basket.

6. Proof: Place each basket in a non-perforated plastic bag and refrigerate overnight. The next morning, 12-14 hours after the loaves went into the fridge, they should be ready to bake, straight from the fridge.

7. Preheat: At least 45 minutes prior to baking, put a rack in the middle of the oven and put 2 Dutch ovens on the rack with their lids on. Preheat the oven to 475℉. If you only have 1 Dutch oven, put the second loaf into the refrigerator about 20 minutes before baking the first loaf and bake the loaves sequentially, giving the Dutch oven a 5-minute reheat after removing the first loaf.

8. Bake: Invert the proofed loaf onto a lightly floured countertop, keeping in mind that the top of the loaf will be the side that was facing down while it was rising -- the seam side. Score with a sharp knife or a lame.

Remove the preheated Dutch oven from the oven, remove the lid, and carefully place the loaf in the Dutch oven -- seam side up. Cover and bake for 30 minutes. Remove the lid, turn the heat down to 425℉, then bake for an additional ~20 minutes, until medium-dark brown to very-dark brown all around the loaf. Check after 15 minutes of baking uncovered in case your oven runs hot.

Remove the Dutch oven and carefully tilt it to turn the loaf out. Let cool on a rack or set the loaf on its side so air can circulate around it. Let the loaf rest for at least 20 minutes before slicing.

Notes

Yields two ~680g loaves of bread.
